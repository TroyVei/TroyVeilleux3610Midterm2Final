Best Armenian Genocide Movies

Scroll down or use the navigation bar at the top right of the page to find out the movies that best educate on and depict the tragedies of the Armenian Genocide.

Rank 1

The Promise

"The Promise" is an amazing film that combines historical context to illustrate the horrors of the Armenian Genocide for viewers. By highlighting the atrocities committed during this dark historical period, the film brings attention to a topic that is often overlooked. Christian Bale's status and ability as an actor adds credibility and emotional intensity to the movie. His role as an American journalist caught in the genocide allows the audience to empathize, making the historical incidents resonate more strongly. "The Promise" effectively uses great storytelling mixed with historical truth to bring the Armenian Genocide to the general public.

Rank 2

Ararat

"Ararat" , directed by Atom Egoyan, teaches people about the Armenian Genocide by telling a powerful story that mixes real history with fiction. The movie attempts to give the viewers a glimpse of the horrors of the genocide, and how the truth about the genocide can be denied or changed. Through interesting characters and a strong story, "Ararat" makes it easier for people to understand and remember this important part of history.
